AstroHaven Data Cited in IAU Minor Planet Circular No. 137894

By: Research Team·8 min read·Asteroid Photometry

In a milestone moment for distributed online astronomy, photometric data submitted by AstroHaven members has been formally cited in International Astronomical Union Minor Planet Circular No. 137894 — validating the scientific contribution of coordinated amateur observation networks.

What is the IAU Minor Planet Circular?

The Minor Planet Circular (MPC) is an official publication of the International Astronomical Union's Minor Planet Center, based at the Center for Astrophysics at Harvard & Smithsonian. Published continuously since 1947, the MPC is the primary scientific record for newly discovered and confirmed asteroids, comets, and other small solar system bodies.

Key fact: Asteroid 2025 XK7 was first identified by a professional survey telescope in December 2025. AstroHaven members contributed follow-up photometric observations within 48 hours of the discovery announcement.

How AstroHaven Captured the Data

The asteroid 2025 XK7, a roughly 180-metre diameter rocky body classified as an Apollo-type near-Earth asteroid, was flagged by the Catalina Sky Survey on December 14, 2025. Within hours of the Minor Planet Center's discovery alert, Research Director Dr. James Okafor coordinated an emergency observation campaign across six AstroHaven members on three continents.

Why This Matters

The citation of distributed amateur data in an official IAU publication challenges a longstanding assumption: that meaningful planetary science requires expensive professional infrastructure. Our members used personal telescopes ranging from 8-inch Dobsonians to 12-inch SCTs — equipment accessible to any serious amateur.

The full citation reads: "Photometric observations of 2025 XK7 were contributed by the AstroHaven Astronomy Club observer network, covering three apparitions between 2025 December 16–18 UT."
