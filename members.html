Our People

Leadership of
AstroHaven

Meet the four core members who guide our global community of 54 astronomers.

👩‍🚀
Club President
Dr. Layla Al-Rashid
Research Division Lead · Variable Star Specialist
Founding member and club president. Holds a PhD in astrophysics and leads the overall strategic direction of AstroHaven. She has led Project Meridian for three years, contributing to peer-reviewed publications on RR Lyrae period variations — work cited by researchers at ESO and the IAU.

Her vision of building an online astronomy club that produces genuinely rigorous science shaped every division and protocol we have today. She personally reviews all AAVSO data submissions and mentors new research members.
👨‍💼
Vice President & Platform Architect
Thomas Müller
Operations Lead · Software Engineer
Thomas is the technological backbone of AstroHaven. He designed and built our custom online collaboration platform from scratch — the infrastructure that allows 54 members to observe, communicate, and share data seamlessly in real time.

His Phase 2 platform upgrade enabled fully synchronised multi-site imaging sessions and remote telescope coordination, features previously only available to professional observatories. He runs the Technical Division and mentors members interested in astronomical software development.
🧑‍🔬
Research Director
Dr. James Okafor
Pioneer Member · Exoplanet Transit Specialist
A PhD astrophysicist who joined AstroHaven after our data quality convinced him that online amateur clubs could do professional-grade science. He now leads Project Horizon — our multi-site exoplanet transit observation programme.

Dr. Okafor oversees all research methodology, peer-review standards, and AAVSO reporting protocols. Under his direction, Project Horizon achieved its first confirmed detection of WASP-17b with 1.3 mmag photometric precision.
👩‍💻
Outreach & Communications Director
Amina Osei
Outreach Division Lead · Education Specialist
The driving force behind AstroHaven's public presence and educational programmes. Amina manages the club's virtual star parties, school outreach initiatives, newsletter, and content strategy across all platforms.

Since joining in 2021, she has personally introduced over 500 people to astronomy through live-streamed events and educational video series. Her "Eyes on the Universe" school programme has now reached 1,000 students across 14 schools in 9 countries.
