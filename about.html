Who We Are

About
AstroHaven

Founded in 2020 by a group of passionate stargazers, AstroHaven has grown from a small online meetup into a recognised institution in the global amateur astronomy community.

Our History

Our Story

AstroHaven was born in the digital space in November 2020, when five amateur astronomers scattered across different continents decided to connect their telescopes and their curiosity. What began as informal monthly online meetups rapidly evolved into something none of us anticipated.

Today we are a 54-member strong global organisation with research programs contributing to international databases, and an outreach program that has introduced over 3,000 people to the wonders of astronomy — all online.

Our founding vision was simple: create a digital space where scientific rigour meets genuine wonder. We refused to be a casual chat group. From day one, we structured ourselves like a small institution — with divisions, research protocols, and a commitment to quality output.

This philosophy has attracted members from diverse backgrounds — physicists, engineers, teachers, artists, and curious minds from every corner of the world — united by the oldest human impulse: looking up.

Journey

Our Timeline

NOVEMBER 2020
Founding Night
Five founding members connect online for the first unofficial AstroHaven observation session, sharing live telescope views across three different continents.
MARCH 2021
Official Establishment
AstroHaven is formally established as an online astronomy club with 28 founding members across 12 countries. First constitution drafted and divisions established.
SEPTEMBER 2021
Platform Launch
Launched our custom online collaboration platform, enabling shared observation sessions, data pooling, and automated multi-telescope coordination.
FEBRUARY 2022
Project Meridian Launches
Launch of our flagship variable star monitoring program. First formal research proposal submitted to AAVSO for inclusion in their international observer network.
OCTOBER 2023
First Peer-Reviewed Publication
Club data contributes to a peer-reviewed paper on RR Lyrae period variations, with three members listed as co-authors in a major astrophysics journal.
2026 — PRESENT
54 Members & Growing
Now one of the most active online astronomy clubs globally, with members contributing to science every clear night.
What We Believe

Core Values

🔬
Scientific Integrity
Every observation is rigorously documented and every claim is evidence-based. We hold ourselves to publication-grade standards because real science demands nothing less — even in an amateur setting. Our data is timestamped, calibrated, and cross-checked before submission to any external database.
🌍
Radical Inclusion
The universe belongs to everyone. Being an online club means geography, socioeconomic background, or lack of expensive equipment is no barrier. We actively work to make serious astronomy accessible to all — from subsidised membership to loaner software licences.
🤝
Collaborative Spirit
We believe the best discoveries happen when diverse minds — across continents and time zones — work together. Our distributed structure is not a limitation; it is our greatest scientific strength, enabling continuous 24-hour global sky coverage.
💡
Perpetual Curiosity
We never stop asking questions. That impulse — to look up, to wonder, to seek understanding — is the common thread connecting every member of this community, from PhD astrophysicists to enthusiastic beginners seeing Jupiter through a telescope for the first time.
✨
Wonder as Purpose
At our core, we are still those five people on a call, sharing telescope views across continents, utterly awestruck by a sky full of stars. That sense of wonder is not a side effect of what we do — it is the whole point.
